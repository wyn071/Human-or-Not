Front Page Investigation

Can You Read the Difference?

Some exhibits were made by real people before generative AI rewired our instincts. Others are modern fabrications crafted to sound or look authentic. Your job is to file the right verdict.

Every round presents a single exhibit from the archive: a passage from literature, scholarship, public documents, or an image circulating as evidence. The challenge is not whether it is old, polished, or beautiful. The challenge is whether it was made by human intention or generated by a model imitating that intention.

The visual tone is deliberately editorial: worn paper, bureau notes, clipped metadata, and a front-page rhythm that makes each guess feel like a filed report. Strong instincts help, but so does noticing texture, specificity, weirdness, over-neat language, and the kind of detail machines often smooth away.
